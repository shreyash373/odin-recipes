Gulab Jamun

Description

It is a traditional Indian sweet

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry milk powder
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter), melted
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup warm milk
  • 1 tablespoon chopped almonds (Optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped pistachio nuts (Optional)
  • 1 tablespoon golden raisins (Optional)
  • 1 pinch ground cardamom
  • 1 quart vegetable oil for deep frying
  • 1 ¼ cups white sugar
  • 7 fluid ounces water
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • 1 pinch ground cardamom

Steps

  1. Combine sugar and water in a saucepan and heat until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Simmer for a few minutes until the syrup is slightly sticky, then add rose water and cardamom.
  3. Mix milk powder, flour, baking powder, and cardamom in a bowl.
  4. Add melted ghee and combine with the dry mixture.
  5. Gradually add warm milk and form a soft dough.
  6. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  7. Divide the dough into small marble-sized portions.
  8. Roll each portion into smooth, crack-free balls.
  9. Heat oil on low-medium flame until moderately hot.
  10. Fry the balls in small batches, stirring gently for even cooking.
  11. Cook until the balls turn deep golden brown.
  12. Remove and drain briefly.
  13. Transfer the hot balls immediately into the warm syrup.
  14. Repeat frying and soaking for the remaining dough balls.
  15. Let the gulab jamuns soak in the syrup for at least 30 minutes.
  16. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with nuts if desired.
